\begin{ChineseAbstract}
在高度复杂且动态变化的不确定环境中，强化学习多机器人系统在协作任务中表现出良好的适应性。而现有多智能体强化学习方法多在高保真仿真环境中训练后再部署至真实机器人，普遍存在 sim-to-real 性能差距。同时，强化学习仍面临训练周期长、多任务泛化能力有限以及探索阶段缺乏安全保障等问题。针对上述挑战，本文围绕不确定环境中的多机器人安全协作控制展开研究，主要工作如下：

（1）针对不确定环境中机器人自主决策过程中的安全问题，本文提出一种由流形安全滤波器、离线 Hamilton–Jacobi 可达性分析及数据驱动扩展卡尔曼滤波器构成的统一框架。流形安全滤波器通过零空间投影将策略动作映射至约束流形切空间，并结合奖励校准机制，引导策略学习内在安全行为；可达性分析基于离线轨迹数据预计算环境相关安全区域，在部署阶段无需实时感知即可自适应调整安全裕度；数据驱动的扩展卡尔曼滤波器利用卷积神经网络学习噪声参数，实现测量协方差对速度相关传感器噪声的自适应建模。实验结果表明，该方法在多个安全强化学习基准任务中几乎实现零约束违背，显著优于对比方法。此外，在移动机器人仿真平台上的零样本部署实验进一步验证了所提框架在实际系统中的良好迁移性与鲁棒性。

（2）针对强化学习在多机器人协同任务中难以同时兼顾策略性能与几何安全约束的问题，提出了一种结合 RMPflow 与强化学习的分层控制方法。该方法通过强化学习对导航与协作行为进行策略优化，并利用 RMPflow 对避障、编队保持等任务进行统一的几何建模与约束合成，使策略输出始终在安全可行空间内调整与更新，从而在复杂环境中实现兼顾安全性与效率的多机器人协同控制。本文在 Safety-Gymnasium 基准环境中部署多智能体编队导航任务，验证不同编队形状下方法的有效性，实验证明所提方法在任务完成度以及安全约束违反上都明显优于对比基线方法，验证了该方法的有效性。

（3）为验证所提出方法在复杂场景中的有效性与可扩展性，构建了面向多机器人强化学习的实验与验证平台。该平台能够在不同环境规模与任务配置下对安全策略进行系统测试，并为多机器人算法的实现、调试与性能评估提供统一的实验基础。通过在该平台上开展多组对比实验，验证了所提方法在提升多机器人系统安全性与协作稳定性方面的有效性。

\ChineseKeywords{约束流形；多智能体；强化学习}
\end{ChineseAbstract}

%% 英文摘要页
\begin{EnglishAbstract}
In highly complex and dynamically changing uncertain environments, reinforcement learning (RL)–based multi-robot systems demonstrate strong adaptability in cooperative tasks. However, most existing multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) approaches are trained in high-fidelity simulation and subsequently deployed on real robots, which commonly leads to a significant sim-to-real performance gap. In addition, RL still faces challenges such as long training cycles, limited generalization across multiple tasks, and a lack of safety guarantees during exploration. To address these issues, this work investigates safe cooperative control for multi-robot systems operating in uncertain environments. The main contributions are as follows:

(1) To ensure safety during autonomous decision-making, a unified framework is proposed that integrates a manifold safety filter, offline Hamilton–Jacobi reachability analysis, and a data-driven extended Kalman filter (EKF). The manifold safety filter projects policy actions onto the tangent space of the constraint manifold via null-space projection and incorporates a reward calibration mechanism to encourage intrinsically safe behaviors. The reachability module pre-computes environment-aware safe regions from offline trajectory data, enabling adaptive safety margins during deployment without relying on real-time sensing. The data-driven EKF employs a convolutional neural network to learn noise parameters, allowing the measurement covariance to adapt to velocity-dependent sensor noise. Experimental results on multiple safe RL benchmark tasks demonstrate near-zero constraint violations and clear performance improvements over baseline methods. Zero-shot deployment on a mobile robot simulation platform further verifies the transferability and robustness of the proposed framework.

(2) To address the difficulty of simultaneously achieving high policy performance and satisfying geometric safety constraints in multi-robot cooperative tasks, a hierarchical control approach that combines RMPflow with reinforcement learning is proposed. In this method, reinforcement learning optimizes navigation and coordination policies, while RMPflow provides unified geometric modeling and constraint composition for tasks such as obstacle avoidance and formation maintenance. This ensures that policy outputs are continuously adjusted within a safe and feasible action space, enabling efficient and safe multi-robot cooperation in complex environments. The approach is evaluated on multi-agent formation navigation tasks in Safety-Gymnasium, where experiments across different formation shapes demonstrate superior task completion and fewer safety violations compared with baseline methods.

(3) To validate the effectiveness and scalability of the proposed methods in complex scenarios, an experimental and verification platform for multi-robot reinforcement learning is developed. The platform supports systematic testing of safety strategies under varying environment scales and task configurations, and provides a unified infrastructure for algorithm implementation, debugging, and performance evaluation. Extensive comparative experiments conducted on this platform confirm that the proposed methods significantly improve the safety and cooperative stability of multi-robot systems.

\EnglishKeywords{Constraint Manifold; Multi-agent; Reinforcement Learning}
\end{EnglishAbstract}
